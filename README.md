# Apple Sales Analysis Report

## Overview
This project presents a comprehensive data analysis of Apple's sales performance, product trends, and warranty claims spanning from 2020 to 2024. Utilizing Python and key data science libraries, the analysis aims to provide actionable insights into market performance, product reliability, and strategic growth opportunities.

## Key Findings

*   **Strong Revenue Performance with Exceptional Product Reliability**: Total revenue reached $6.17 billion across 1.04 million transactions, with a remarkably low 2.88% warranty claim rate. This indicates robust market performance coupled with high product quality.

## Strategic Priorities for Sustainable Growth
Based on the analysis, four strategic priorities are recommended:

1.  **Quality Control Investigation**: Immediately investigate the root causes of high warranty claims in the Accessories and Smartphone categories to enhance product reliability and reduce post-sale costs.
2.  **Service Expansion**: Leverage the success of the Subscription Service model (e.g., Apple Music) to drive higher-margin recurring revenue and increase customer lifetime value.
3.  **Market Diversification**: Develop targeted strategies to increase market share in high-potential countries (e.g., China, Japan, Australia) to mitigate geographic risk and reduce reliance on the US market.
4.  **Success Replication**: Analyze the success factors of top-performing products (e.g., Tablets, Services) and apply these lessons across the entire product portfolio to maximize revenue potential.

## Data Sources
The analysis utilized the following datasets:

*   `category.csv`: Contains information about product categories.
*   `products.csv`: Details about individual products, including launch dates and prices.
*   `sales.csv`: Records of sales transactions.
*   `stores.csv`: Information about retail store locations.
*   `warranty.csv`: Data on warranty claims.

## Methodology
The data analysis followed these steps:

1.  **Data Loading**: All CSV files were loaded into pandas DataFrames.
2.  **Column Standardization**: Column names were standardized for consistency and ease of merging.
3.  **Data Type Conversion**: Relevant columns, such as `launch_date` and `sale_date`, were converted to datetime objects for time-series analysis.
4.  **Data Merging**: The DataFrames were merged based on common identifiers (`product_id`, `category_id`, `store_id`) to create a comprehensive dataset for analysis.
5.  **Feature Engineering**: A `revenue` column was calculated by multiplying `quantity` by `price`. A `has_warranty_claim` flag was created to identify sales associated with warranty claims.
6.  **Key Performance Indicator (KPI) Calculation**: Metrics such as total revenue, total units sold, total transactions, and overall warranty claim rate were calculated.
7.  **In-depth Analysis**: Further analysis was conducted to understand:
    *   Revenue by product category.
    *   Top-selling products by revenue.
    *   Monthly and yearly sales trends.
    *   Revenue distribution by country.
    *   Warranty claims by product category.
